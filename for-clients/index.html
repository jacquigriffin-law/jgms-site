Bring or send key dates, any court documents, the other party's contact details and a list of urgent questions. A short timeline of relevant events can help make the first meeting more efficient.

Common Questions

What new clients often ask

What should I bring or send before an appointment?

Any court documents, relevant correspondence, financial documents (for property or family matters) and a brief timeline of major events. Even a rough set of notes is helpful — you don't need everything organised perfectly before the first call.

Can appointments be remote?

Yes. Phone and video appointments are available and are often perfectly suitable for initial consultations, advice and ongoing matters. Jacqui uses phone, Zoom, FaceTime and other communication tools depending on what works best.

What are the fees?

This depends on the type and complexity of your matter. Jacqui offers fixed-fee packages for suitable work and tailored estimates for more complex matters. The fees page has more detail, and costs will be explained clearly at your first appointment.
