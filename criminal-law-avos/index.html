Criminal law and AVOs

Support for AVO-related matters, domestic violence proceedings and criminal law issues requiring prompt attention in NSW and the NT.

AVOs and related matters

Advice about the practical implications of an apprehended violence order, upcoming court dates and the steps that may need to be taken before or at a hearing. Jacqui holds the NSW Legal Aid domestic violence panel.

Criminal proceedings

Guidance on process, preparation and representation where criminal law matters arise. Understanding the charge, the likely process and the practical options is usually the first priority.

Urgent first steps

Early action can matter significantly in criminal and AVO matters. Gathering documents, understanding conditions, confirming court dates and knowing what not to do are often the most important starting points.

Accessible advice

Phone and video appointments mean you can get advice quickly when timing is important. Jacqui is available by mobile for initial guidance on urgent matters.

Dealing with an urgent matter?

If you have an upcoming court date, have been served with an AVO, or need urgent first-step advice, call directly. Early attention can make a significant difference to how a matter unfolds.
