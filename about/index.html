About Jacqui Griffin

Solicitor, LLB (UNSW), MA (UNSW), BEd (Flinders University) · Admitted to practice in NSW 1999 · 25+ years' legal experience

Background

From teacher to solicitor — a career built on helping people

Jacqui Griffin has been working as a lawyer for over 25 years, admitted to practice in New South Wales in 1999. Before entering the law, she spent approximately 17 years as a high school teacher — teaching science, mathematics and physical education in her hometown of Darwin and in Sydney.

That background in education shaped how Jacqui approaches legal work: with a commitment to clear explanation, genuine patience and a focus on helping clients understand what is happening and why. Legal proceedings can be confusing and stressful; Jacqui's aim is to make the process as clear and manageable as possible.

After being admitted, Jacqui worked initially in a suburban firm in Toongabbie, Sydney, doing a broad range of legal work. She then opened her own legal practice, where her focus has been family law, care and protection matters (Children's Court), and domestic violence orders (ADVOs). She also undertakes criminal law work and estate matters including wills and probates.

In recent years Jacqui has placed particular emphasis on assisting self-represented litigants in family law — offering unbundled legal services and fixed-fee family law packages to make legal support genuinely accessible for working people who need help but cannot afford traditional full-service representation.

Jacqui sees clients in person in Sydney, Darwin, or other locations by arrangement. She is available to appear in person at Federal Circuit and Family Court of Australia registries around Australia, and genuinely enjoys the travel that comes with that.

"My focus is on helping people who are going through really difficult times navigate a system that can feel overwhelming. I want clients to leave an appointment with a clearer picture of where they stand and what their options are."

— Jacqui Griffin, Solicitor

Practice Focus

Core areas of practice

Jacqui's practice is focused on areas that involve high stakes and often urgent circumstances — family breakdown, child protection, criminal charges and estate planning.

  • Family law — separation, parenting, property settlement, divorce and child support
  • Unbundled legal services and fixed-fee family law packages for self-represented litigants
  • Care and protection law — Children's Court matters in NSW and the NT
  • Domestic violence orders — ADVOs and related proceedings
  • Criminal law matters requiring prompt attention
  • Wills, powers of attorney, probate and estate administration
  • NSW Legal Aid panels — family law, domestic violence, care and protection, s102NA
  • NT legal aid work

Service approach

Many clients come to Jacqui during the most stressful periods of their lives. The aim is always to give clear, practical advice — explaining what is required now, what can wait, and what the process is likely to look like.

Appointments are available by phone, video, in person in Parramatta or Darwin, or by arrangement elsewhere. Jacqui is also able to appear at court registries around Australia.
