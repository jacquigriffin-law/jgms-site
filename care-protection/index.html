Care and Protection Support

Clear guidance through difficult situations

Care and protection matters are often urgent, highly stressful and deeply personal. The focus is on helping clients understand the process, prepare properly and respond effectively to the issues raised.

These matters typically move very quickly. Early legal advice can assist with understanding documents, court expectations, preparation for hearings and keeping the focus on practical next steps.

Support is available for:

  • Children's Court proceedings
  • Response to Department of Communities or equivalent agency involvement
  • Case planning, preparation and court attendance
  • Communication with agencies, caseworkers and the other parties
  • Interim and final order proceedings
  • Support for parents, carers and family members

NSW Legal Aid — care and protection

Jacqui holds the NSW Legal Aid panel for care and protection matters. If you think you may be eligible for legal aid assistance in a Children's Court matter, this can be discussed at your initial appointment.

How early advice helps

Understanding documents

Care and protection matters often involve complex government documents, reports and court applications. Early advice on what these mean and what is required in response can be crucial.

Court preparation

Children's Court proceedings move through stages. Knowing what to expect at each stage, what documents to have ready and how hearings are likely to run helps clients feel prepared rather than overwhelmed.

Practical next steps

These matters are stressful. Jacqui's approach is to identify the most important action for right now, explain why, and give a realistic picture of how the process is likely to unfold.

Need advice quickly?

Care and protection matters often require urgent attention. If you have received documents from the Department or have an upcoming court date, call directly — early advice is almost always valuable in these matters.
