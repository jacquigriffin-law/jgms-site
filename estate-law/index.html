Estate Planning

Planning and protection

Estate planning is about making your intentions clear and protecting the people who depend on you. A properly drafted will and associated documents can prevent significant difficulty for your family later.

Support is available for a wide range of estate matters, from straightforward wills and powers of attorney through to more complex estate planning and administration.

  • Wills and estate planning
  • Probate and letters of administration
  • Powers of attorney
  • Appointments of enduring guardianship
  • Executor and estate administration support

When legal help matters most

  • Blended families or second relationships
  • Business interests or family trusts
  • Executor duties and probate steps
  • Questions about capacity, witnessing or validity
  • Updating an existing will after a major life event

Common Matters

Frequently asked topics

Executor support

Executors may need help locating assets, arranging valuations, applying for probate, handling outstanding liabilities and making distributions to beneficiaries. Jacqui can assist at any stage of the process.

When to review your will

A will should be reviewed after major life events — marriage or de facto relationships, divorce, having children, significant changes in assets, or family circumstances changing. It should also be reviewed periodically even without a specific trigger.

Tailored documents

The right document structure depends on personal circumstances. Estate planning should be tailored to your actual situation rather than assumed from a standard template. An appointment is usually the best starting point.

Phone or video appointments

Estate planning appointments can often be conducted entirely by phone or video, which can be more convenient for clients who are unwell, busy or based outside Parramatta or Darwin.

Jacqui offers mobile appointments where appropriate for clients who cannot easily travel.
